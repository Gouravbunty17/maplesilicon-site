// Argus detection engine

Argus is the 5-layer
engine inside Maple Shield.

Maple Shield is the system. Argus is the detection and intelligence engine inside it. The stack moves from passive sensing intake to operator-ready evidence without collapsing the whole product into a single model.

Layer 01
Passive sensing intake

Ingest video and adjacent sensor feeds in a workflow that preserves passive operation as the starting assumption.

Layer 02
Detection and classification

Surface airborne objects quickly enough for live operator use, with the detection layer tuned for practical review rather than demo-only output.

Layer 03
Track continuity

Maintain identity through clutter, occlusion, and movement so the operator sees a coherent track instead of a flickering sequence of detections.

Layer 04
Alerting and scoring

Prioritize likely threats, reduce noise, and keep the human decision chain readable under pressure.

Layer 05
Replay and intelligence

Preserve event history, evidence, and replayable incident context for review, tuning, and post-event intelligence.

// Why passive matters

Why passive matters
before everything else.

For the environments Maple Shield is aimed at, the sensing doctrine matters as much as the model quality. Passive operation is not a side detail; it is the reason the system belongs in the conversation.

01
EMCON-sensitive environments

In Latvia-style EMCON conditions, passive detection helps preserve awareness without creating an unnecessary signal.

02
Remote and Arctic operations

In Arctic and other remote deployments, infrastructure is limited, weather is unforgiving, and the system has to stay useful without leaning on a rich backend.

03
Operator trust and evidence

Passive sensing only matters if operators can trust the alert flow, review the event, and explain why the system raised the signal in the first place.
