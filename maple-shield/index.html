Maple Shield / edge AI airspace awareness

Detection, tracking, and operator clarity at the edge.

Maple Shield is Maple Silicon Inc.'s platform for drone detection, persistent multi-target tracking, alerting, and replayable incident intelligence in authorized environments. The focus is the software layer that makes an airspace monitoring system actually usable by operators.

System layers with a clear job to do.

Maple Shield is being shaped as a full sensing and operator layer. The opportunity is not a single dramatic component. It is the integrated stack that sees, tracks, scores, and explains.

Layer 01

Detection engine

Computer vision for live video and edge sensor feeds, tuned to surface airborne objects quickly enough for real-time workflows.

Layer 02

Persistent tracking

Stable track identities, continuity across frames, and the ability to maintain operator trust even when clutter and occlusion appear.

Layer 03

Alerting and scoring

Risk-aware operator cues that prioritize what matters, reduce noise, and keep human decision-making readable under pressure.

Layer 04

Replay and evidence

Incident history, replayable logs, and a structured audit trail that turns raw detections into useful post-event intelligence.

Principles behind the product.

The product direction is being kept disciplined: lawful detection and awareness first, edge deployment by default, and a system design that can evolve without turning into a hardware-first trap.

01

Software-first moat

The durable value sits in perception, track continuity, event intelligence, and integration, not in trying to start with expensive custom hardware.

02

Edge-first runtime

Latency, resilience, and constrained deployment matter. Maple Shield is being positioned for the node closest to the sensor, not only the server furthest away.

03

Operator-grade interface

A serious system must produce understandable alerts, replayable evidence, and confidence that the workflow helps humans act instead of overwhelming them.

How the workflow is meant to feel.

The architecture is not just model-in, box-out. The experience should move from sensor feed to trusted action with a chain that remains understandable at every step.

01

Sense

Ingest video or adjacent sensor data, perform real-time detection, and establish an initial track candidate with minimal delay.

02

Interpret

Stabilize the track, maintain identity, score the event against zone or scenario context, and filter away alert noise.

03

Record

Log the event, preserve evidence, and make the incident replayable so teams can validate performance and understand what happened.
